A Cinephile's Guide to New York City

From "The Seven-Year Itch" to "Home Alone," New York has enticed directors and moviegoers alike. Check out some of the most iconic NYC locations in film history!

By Claudia Irizarry Aponte

Walk down any block in New York City, and chances are you’ll recognize it from a scene in a movie. While some landmarks are obvious choices for a film—the Empire State Building, Central Park, and Grand Central Terminal, to name a few, other become landmarks simply because they appear in a film, like the site of Marilyn Monroe’s notorious upskirt shot from The Seven-Year Itch at 52nd Street and Lexington Avenue.

“The city seen from the Queensboro Bridge is always the city seen for the first time, in its first wild promise of all the mystery and the beauty in the world.” --The Great Gatsby

Filmmakers love capturing New York it all its grit and glory, depictions which have inspired sightseeing and scavenger-hunt pilgrimages and entire generations of dreamers to settle down in NYC. And who could blame them?
